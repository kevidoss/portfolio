GHENT

NEWS

Ghent is a city and a municipality in the Flemish Region of Belgium. It is the capital and largest city of the East Flanders province and after Antwerp the largest municipality of Belgium. The city started as a settlement at the confluence of the Rivers Scheldt and Leie and in the Late Middle Ages became one of the largest and richest cities of northern Europe with some 50,000 people in 1300. It is a port and university city.

TO DO IN GHENT

Capitole

The Capitole is a theatre and event hall in the Belgian city of Ghent. The building has 2.000 seats and is located at the Graaf van Vlaanderenplein.

Dr. Guislain Museum

The Museum Dr. Guislain is a museum in Ghent where the history of psychiatry is shown by utilities, arts and research. The museum opened in 1986.

St Bavo's Cathedral

St Bavo's Cathedral - Home of Ghents Alterpiece (Het Lam Gods) is a 89m-tall Gothic cathedral. It is the seat of the diocese of Ghent and it is named for Saint Bavo of Ghent.

House of Alijn

The House of Alijn is a museum in Ghent. The collection inside shows the daily life of the 20th century. The museum is located in the Kinderen van Alijnshospitaal, a former house of God.

SMAK

Stedelijk Museum voor Actuele Kunst - The museum of modern arts in Ghent is renowned both for its permanent collection and for its provocative exhibitions.

Museum Of Fine Arts

Museum voor Schone Kunsten - The museum of fine arts in Ghent holds a large permanent collection of art from the Middle Ages until the mid 20th century. The collection focuses on Flemish Art, but also has several European paintings.
